Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Income

In the scatter plot chart above, we are investigating the relationships between demographic information and health risk (lack of healthcare vs. poverty rate). The data collected was from the U.S. Census Bureau's American Community Survey and the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System for the year of 2014. Each point in the scatter represents a state (hover over point for more information). The scatter plot shows a positive correlation between rate of lack of healthcare and poverty.

The trend shows that as rate of poverty increases, so does the rate of lack of healthcare. Some of the southern states lead in the trend and that can be explained by the affordability of healthcare product products whereas states like New York and California have high income rate that can afford the healthcare product. The outlier in this scatter plot would be Texas. States with the similar rate of poverty to Texas would have around 17% rate of lack of healthcare product. And Massachusetts has the lowest poverty and lack of healthcare rate which can be explained that it has passed a health care reform law in 2006 with the aim of providing health insurance to nearly all of its residents.
